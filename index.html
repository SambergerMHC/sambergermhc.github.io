GDP vs Population Change of 2013

Examining If Asia and Africa Will be Able to Grow Econimically with a Rapidly Growing Population

The United Nations Department of Economic and Social Affairs predicts that by 2050, Africa and Asia will comprise 78% of the world's 9.5 billion population. However, countries be able to economically sustain a rapid increase in population with a healty annual growth in Gross Domestic Product (GDP)? Comparing population change as a percentage and GDP change as a percentage from data downloaded from the UN Data Exchange, Asia appears cabable of accomidating a growing population, while Africa does not. As GDP simply measures the total value of goods and services purchased and produced in a country, larger populations lead to higher GDPs. As Africa and Asia were the two continents taht experienced the largest growth in population in 2013, countries in Africa and Asia would ideally have the greatest GDP growth. Africa's population skyrocketed in 2013. Half of all countries in Africa are in the highest quantile, and all but six are in the two highest quantiles. However, despite massive population growth across Africa in 2013, only about half of African countries were above the mean in GDP increase. Asia's future seems far more positive. Central and South Western Asian countries had for the most part,above average population growth, while the majority of South East Asian countries hovered around the mean. However, all but five countries in Asia were in the top two quantiles of GDP growth.
